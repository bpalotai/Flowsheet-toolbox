What is Parameter Sampling?

Parameter sampling is a technique used to explore the design space of a simulation model by systematically varying input parameters. It helps in understanding parameter relationships, uncertainty quantification, and model validation.

Benefits
  • Efficiently explore large parameter spaces
  • Identify critical parameters and their effects
  • Support surrogate model training with diverse data
  • Enable uncertainty and sensitivity analysis
  • Generate data for Monte Carlo simulations
Available Sampling Methods
Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS) Available

Stratified sampling method that ensures better coverage of the parameter space than random sampling.

Random Sampling Available

Simple random sampling from parameter ranges with uniform distribution.

Full Factorial Sampling Available

Evaluates all possible combinations of parameter values at specified levels.

Monte Carlo Sampling Available

Random sampling with specified probability distributions for each parameter.

Sobol Sequence Coming Soon

Low-discrepancy sequence that provides excellent space-filling properties.
